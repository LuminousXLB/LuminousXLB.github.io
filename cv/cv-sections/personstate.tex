\lettersection{Academic Interest}

I have a great interest in blockchain technology and implementation of security protocols on IoT platforms.

Blockchain is a technology that leverages cryptography theory and distributed storage technology, which makes it decentralized. These features ensure that the data on it cannot be tampered and less likely to lose, hence it may do well in documenting the facts. At the same time, user privacy may be well protected with the help of asymmetric cryptosystem.

Many things are still to be done to promote the application of blockchain technology. We need reasonable mechanisms to claim ownership or inherit others property on blockchain without increasing the risk of leaking user privacy. Without such mechanisms, people would doubt cryptocurrencies' safety and the government would also deny the legitimacy of them for it's hard to tax on. Approaches shall be proposed to link blockchain together with realistic world in specific applications as well. Although data on blockchains can't be tampered, we have few means to verify the authenticity of what was uploaded, which makes it difficult to check the integrity of data on the chain. Only if we could make sure that the input of blockchain is valid, can we look forward to people's applying it to realistic world,

There seem few things that we can do better using blockchain technology at present. But I believe that there will be some new situations which couldn't be better but to build a system on blockchains in the future. While blockchain was built on cryptography and distributed consensus algorithms, the improvement of blockchain would also benefit the development of cryptography engineering and distributed computing technology.

Apart from blockchain, implementing cryptographic algorithms and security protocols are also fantastic jobs. Efficient and secure implementation of cryptographic algorithms and security protocols is the cornerstone of modern network technology. In the application of IoT, where privacy matters a lot, it's more important. So, I'm willing to dedicate to improve the security of IoT equipment.

In LoCCS, the lab I currently work in, I have learned some basic knowledge about cryptography theory, network essentials and basic concepts of Bitcoin. We are still being trained on other topics about mainstream cryptocurrencies and blockchain technologies, such as Ethereum, Zillqa, EOS.io and so on. And I complete a project independently to integrate digest method SM3 to a modified version of OpenSSL. These are far from enough, and I'm always on the way to learn more and do more.
