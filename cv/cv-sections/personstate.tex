\lettersection{Academic Interest}

I am deeply interested in blockchain technology and implementation of security protocols on IoT platforms.

Blockchain leverages cryptography theory and distributed storage technology to ensure its decentralization and that the data on it cannot be tampered. Both features make it born with disaster tolerance and may do well in documenting the facts. At the same time, the privacy of blockchain's users may be well protected with the support of asymmetric cryptosystem.

Many things are still to be done to promote the application of blockchain technology. The strong privacy characteristic makes Bitcoin broadly used in illegal business, ransomwares as an example. In daily life, by contrast, common people rarely keep it and use it to make deals. Not only do the government deny the legitimacy of cryptocurrencies, people also doubt its safety for its lack of a reasonable mechanisms to claim his ownership or inherit others property. Although data on blockchains can't be tampered, we have few means to verify the authenticity of what was uploaded, i.e. approaches shall be proposed to link blockchain together with realistic world in specific applications.

Although there seem few things that we can do better using blockchain technology at present, it is believed that there will be some new situations which couldn't be better but to build a system on blockchains in the future. While blockchain was built on the base of cryptography and distributed consensus algorithms, the improvement of blockchain would benefit the development of cryptography engineering and distributed computing technology.

Apart from blockchain, implementing cryptographic algorithms and security protocols are also fantastic jobs. Efficiency and security of the engineering implementation of cryptographic algorithms and security protocols is the cornerstone of modern network technology. It's more and more important with the wide application of IoT, which is greatly closer to people's privacy. So, I'm willing to dedicate to improve the security of IoT equipment.

In Loccs, as referred above, I have learned some basic knowledge about cryptography theory, network essentials and rough but complete developer document for Bitcoin. We are still being trained on other topics about mainstream cryptocurrencies and blockchain technologies, such as Ethereum, Zillqa, EOS.io, etc. For practice, I complete the implementation of digest algorithms SM3, which has been integrated to OpenSSL though privately.
